PRELIM REFLECTION

End of Prelim Term

Overall Experience

Looking back, I can honestly say that this prelim gave me something I never expected. At first, I thought everything would be so formal and strict—just lectures, deadlines, and pressure. But as the days went on, that idea completely changed. I realized that learning doesn't always have to be so serious; there can also be laughter, sharing of knowledge, and different activities that make the lessons more engaging. Ma'am Gornez really made a difference. She wasn't just an excellent instructor she was also someone who understood us and knew how to create a classroom environment where we felt comfortable to participate and even make mistakes.

Because of this, I can say that my prelim ended on a good note. I gained more than just grades, I gained realizations. I learned how important our culture is, especially the role of Indigenous Peoples and the laws that protect their heritage. These are things I used to only hear about, but now I actually understand and appreciate. Of course, I won't lie, there were moments of nervousness, especially with all the activities we had to accomplish. But in the end, it was all worth it. Not only did I learn a lot, but I also got to know some of my new classmates better. This course brought us together, and I'm thankful for that.

Key Learnings

One of the biggest lessons I will carry with me is the importance of advance study. I realized that if I really want to keep up with the discussion and understand the lessons deeply, I need to prepare ahead of time. It makes a huge difference when you already have an idea of what will be discussed, you can participate more and learn more.

Another thing I learned is the value of sharing knowledge. Hindi puro sarili lang. There were moments when I knew something that my classmates didn't, and I was able to share it with them. At the same time, there were also times when they were the ones who helped me understand a topic better. Learning becomes richer and more meaningful when it is shared. It builds connection, and it reminds us that we are not alone in this journey.

Looking Forward

As we move forward to the next part of this course, I am hoping for more fun and meaningful activities. I want to keep learning, not just from our lessons but also from the people around me. Sana ganito pa rin yung atmosphere, yung may tawanan, may kwentuhan, pero marami pa ring natututunan. And of course, sa exam ng Wednesday, sana kayanin. I'll do my best and bring with me everything Ma'am has taught us.

Slogan for Peace

March 12, 2026

Situation or Experience

After ng reporting at long quiz noong mga nakaraang meeting, ang sunod naming activity ngayong araw ay ang paggawa ng Slogan na may temang nagsusulong ng kapayapaan sa pagitan ng iba't ibang Tao, Kultura, Behavoir. Kailangan naming ilapat ang lahat ng aming natutunan tungkol sa Mass media at sa Cultural studies theory sa pagkakaisa. Ang isinulat ko sa aking slogan ay "Cultural Difference do not make divide: It makes us stronger, Respect one another for a better tomorrow." Medyo alangan nga lang ako noong una kasi alam kong hindi ako magaling sa pag-drawing, at nahihiya akong baka hindi maganda ang maging kinalabasan ng aking output kumpara sa iba. Pero sabi ko sa sarili ko, ibibigay ko na lang ang aking best. Buti na lang at tinulungan ako ni Noreene—siya ang nagbigay sa akin ng layout kung paano iguhit ang mapa ng Pilipinas, at doon ko na isinulat ang aking message. Hindi lang iyon, pinaganda at pinag-isipan ko rin nang mabuti ang mensahe para sa kapayapaan na isinulat ko sa likod ng oslo paper, para kahit papaano, maiparating ko nang maayos ang aking advocacy tungkol sa paggalang sa isa't isa. kahit si Dave ay di rin masyado daw maalam mag drawing pero nong nakita ko ang gawa niya, maganda naman ito.

Emotional and Cognitive Response

Masaya ako kahit papaano dahil natapos ko ang aking slogan kahit na hindi ako mahusay sa visual arts. Medyo kinakabahan pa rin kung magugustuhan ba ni Ma'am namin ang aking gawa, pero mas matimbang ang fulfillment na may natapos ako at may nakasama akong tumulong sa akin. Na-appreciate ko talaga si Noreene dahil hindi niya ako hinayaang mag-isa at nagbigay siya ng idea para maging maganda ang magiging resulta. Kahit nahihirapan ako sa pag-drawing, naramdaman kong hindi ako nag-iisa at may karamay ako sa bawat hamon na dumarating. Iyon pala, ang tunay na diwa ng activity na ito ay hindi lang tungkol sa galing sa pagguhit, kundi sa mensaheng hatid mo at kung paano ka nakikipagkapwa habang ginagawa ito.

SEL Insight

Ang sitwasyong ito ay nagpatibay ng aking self-awareness at social awareness. Napagtanto ko na kahit may mga bagay tayong hindi natin forte tulad ng pag-drawing, hindi ito hadlang upang maging makabuluhan ang ating paticipation. Sa tulong ni Noreene, natutunan kong tanggapin ang aking kahinaan at hayaan ang iba na tumulong sa akin. Ipinakita rin nito sa akin na sa kabila ng pagkakaiba-iba natin—sa talento, kakayahan, at pananaw—maaari pa rin tayong magkaisa para sa iisang layunin: ang magkaroon ng mapayapang samahan at maipahayag ang mensahe ng paggalang sa bawat isa. Natutunan kong hindi hadlang ang kakulangan sa isang aspeto upang hindi ka makapag-ambag, at mahalaga ang pagtutulungan upang mapagtagumpayan ang anumang hamon.

Personal Reflection

In this experience, I understood even more that we don't need to be perfect at everything. What matters is that you give your whole heart to what you're doing, and that you are open to help coming from others. This activity reminded me that the true essence of peace starts with small things—in accepting ourselves and others, despite our differences. Just as important as the message of my slogan, 'Respect one another for a better tomorrow,' it is equally important to live it out in my daily interactions with others. I may not be the best in class when it comes to drawing, but I know my contribution has value, and that is what matters most of all.
